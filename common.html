Common Transposable Elements

Type 1 / RNA Transposons

LTR transposable elements

LTRs are identical sequences of DNA that appear on either end of a series of genes. Each LTR sequence is around a couple hundred base pairs long.

LTR retrotransposons are usually over 5 kilobases long, and in between the LTRS there are genes that can be transcribed to retrovirus genes "gag" and "pol."

These retrotransposons can also insert copies of itself into other parts of the genome.

Gag

Interact with other retrotransposon transcripts to form virus-like structures

Pol

Include reverse transcriptase enzymes that carry out the reverse transcription of retrotransposon DNA

Non-LTR transposable elements

These are similar to LTR transposons in that they also contain genes for reverse transcriptase and are found in the human genome

However, they have shorter repeats that have an inverse order of bases adjacent to one another, as opposed to LTR where it is just one sequence repeating itself

Common RNA Transposon Elements

ALU

A non-LTR element that is believed to have emerged with primates around 65 million years ago, but it has turned into the most prevalent of the transposable elements

Since they are prevalent in humans and primates but not others, it is used in researching evolutionary behaviors [2]

LINE1 (Long Interspersed Element-1)

A non-LTR element that is known for its hypomethylation, which is associated with the spreading of cancers, which serves as an indicator for cancer risk. This retrotransposon is being used to research and better understand the behavior and spread of cancers[3]
